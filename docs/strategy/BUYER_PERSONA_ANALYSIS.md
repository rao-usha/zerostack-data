# Nexdata Buyer Persona Analysis

**Date:** April 6, 2026
**Purpose:** Detailed buyer persona, workflow analysis, and sales playbook for Nexdata

---

## Target Buyer: Mid-Market PE Firm ($1-5B AUM)

### Why Mid-Market?

- ~2,000 firms in this segment — large enough market, underserved by incumbents
- PitchBook is retreating upmarket (laid off mid-market sales staff in Nov 2025)
- Budget: $150K-$500K/yr on data tools — meaningful but not bottomless
- Decision-making: Faster than mega-funds (2-4 months vs. 6-12 months)
- Pain: Acutely feel the cost of stale data and manual processes
- Team size: 5-15 investment professionals — right size for land-and-expand

---

## Persona 1: The Champion — Deal Sourcing VP/Principal

**Demographics:**
- 28-35 years old, 5-10 years in PE
- Previously at a bulge bracket bank or Big 3 consulting firm
- MBA from a top 15 school
- Title: VP, Principal, or Senior Associate

**Daily workflow (60-70 hr/week, 80+ during live deals):**

| Time | Activity | Tools Used | Pain Level |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| 8:30 AM | Triage 40+ emails — banker teasers, portfolio updates, LP comms | Outlook | Medium |
| 9:00 AM | Review new deal opportunities against investment thesis | PitchBook, CapIQ, Excel | HIGH |
| 10:00 AM | Deep research on 2-3 target companies | PitchBook, Google, SEC EDGAR, LinkedIn | HIGH |
| 11:30 AM | Build/update financial model for active deal | Excel (LBO model) | Medium |
| 1:00 PM | Team meeting — discuss pipeline, prioritize targets | PowerPoint, DealCloud | Medium |
| 2:00 PM | Due diligence work on active deal | Multiple sources, manual assembly | HIGH |
| 4:00 PM | Management calls or site visits (if live deal) | Phone/Zoom | Low |
| 5:00 PM | Write IC memo or deal screening summary | PowerPoint, Word | HIGH |
| 7:00 PM | Catch up on industry news, prepare for tomorrow | WSJ, PE Hub, newsletters | Low |

**Time allocation:**
- ~40% screening/research that leads nowhere (80 companies screened per 1 investment)
- ~30% active deal execution (modeling, DD, IC memos)
- ~20% portfolio monitoring
- ~10% admin/LP support

**What they hate:**
- "I spent 4 hours researching a company that turned out to be a non-starter"
- "PitchBook's revenue data is 5 years old — I have to verify everything"
- "I'm searching 5 different databases to answer one question"
- "Every competitor sees the same banker teasers I do"
- "I built an Excel tracker for our add-on target list but it's always stale"

**What they wish they had:**
- "Show me companies in [sector] in [geography] that are growing fast, have aging ownership, and haven't been approached by other PE firms yet"
- "Give me a first-pass DD memo on this company in 10 minutes using public data"
- "Alert me when a portfolio company's competitor starts hiring aggressively"

**What gets their attention:**
- Job posting intelligence as a leading indicator (genuinely novel — nobody else has it)
- Automated DD memo in 60 seconds vs. 4 hours of manual work
- Data from 28 sources they currently stitch together manually

**How to activate them:**
1. Show them something on their own pipeline companies they didn't know
2. Run a "proof of value" on 5 companies from their active pipeline
3. Give them data that makes their next IC memo look exceptional
4. Arm them with ROI talking points: "This saves 2 hours per company screened, 40 companies/quarter = 80 hours = one analyst's quarter"

## Persona 2: The Decision-Maker — COO/CFO or Managing Partner

**Demographics:**
- 40-55 years old, 15-25 years in PE/finance
- Title: COO, CFO, or Managing Partner (at smaller firms)
- Controls the management company budget
- Evaluates vendor spend relative to management fee income

**What they care about:**
- Total cost of data tools vs. value delivered
- Risk of adding another vendor (security, compliance, switching costs)
- Team productivity — can they do more deals with same headcount?
- LP perception — are we using modern tools? (LPs increasingly ask about tech)

**How they evaluate:**
- "What does this replace? What's the net cost after canceling/reducing other tools?"
- "Can I pilot this with 2 seats and expand if it works?"
- "Is the data secure? What are the compliance implications?"
- "Show me the ROI on a real deal we've done"

**Budget context:**
- Management fee: 1.6-2.0% of AUM. A $2B fund = ~$35M/year for everything
- Data tools are a line item competing with salaries, rent, travel
- Under $50K/yr: COO/CFO can approve independently
- $50K-$200K: Partner meeting discussion
- Over $200K: Full partner vote

**What triggers a purchase:**
1. Contract renewal for PitchBook/CapIQ (natural evaluation moment)
2. New fund launch (fresh budget, clean slate)
3. Champion (VP/Principal) builds internal case with proof-of-value data
4. Lost deal attribution ("we missed this signal")
5. LP pressure on tech adoption

## Persona 3: Portfolio Operations Partner

**Demographics:**
- 35-50 years old, often ex-McKinsey/Bain/BCG
- Title: Operating Partner, VP of Portfolio Operations, Head of Value Creation
- Manages 5-15 portfolio companies post-investment
- Reports to Managing Partner; interfaces with portfolio company CEOs

**Daily workflow:**
- Quarterly KPI collection from portfolio companies (manual, email-based)
- Benchmarking portfolio company performance vs. peers
- Exit readiness assessment and prep
- LP reporting (2-6 weeks of manual prep per quarter)
- Coordinating value creation initiatives across portfolio

**What they hate:**
- "I chase 15 portfolio company CFOs every quarter for the same data"
- "Benchmarking is meaningless when Bain charges $300K for a single report"
- "I can't tell which companies are actually exit-ready vs. just looking good"
- "LP reporting takes my entire team offline for 3 weeks every quarter"

**What they wish they had:**
- Real-time portfolio health dashboard with automated KPI collection
- Peer benchmarking against actual comparable companies (not anonymized surveys)
- Exit readiness score that updates automatically
- LP reports that generate themselves from portfolio data

**Nexdata fit:**
- Exit readiness scoring (already built — 6-signal composite)
- Financial benchmarking vs. peer median/P25/P75 (already built)
- Portfolio health scoring (already built — A-F tier)
- People/org chart monitoring (detect leadership departures early)
- Job posting intelligence (hiring velocity as leading indicator of portfolio health)

## The Buying Journey

### Stage 1: Discovery (Weeks 1-2)

**Trigger events:**
- PitchBook contract renewal approaching
- New fund raised, setting up infrastructure
- VP read an article about AI in PE / attended conference
- Lost a competitive deal and wished they had better data

**What happens:**
- Champion (VP/Principal) searches for alternatives
- Reads 2-3 comparison articles, checks G2/peer recommendations
- Asks 2-3 peers at other firms what they use
- Finds Nexdata through content/conference/referral

### Stage 2: Initial Demo (Weeks 2-4)

**What the champion needs to see:**
1. Search for a company they know → get richer data than PitchBook
2. Job posting intelligence on their target → signal they can't get elsewhere
3. Automated DD memo → "I just got in 5 minutes what takes me a day"
4. Score a company → quantified assessment they can use in IC memo

**Demo anti-patterns (avoid):**
- Don't show 28 data sources — show 3 that matter for their sector
- Don't demo site intelligence unless they're infrastructure-focused
- Don't demo the macro cascade — too complex for a first meeting
- Don't talk about technical architecture — talk about outcomes

### Stage 3: Pilot (Weeks 4-8)

**Structure:**
- 2 seats, 30-60 days, full product access
- Pick 5-10 companies from their active pipeline as test subjects
- Dedicated onboarding call + weekly check-in
- Success metrics agreed upfront: time saved per company, data points discovered, signals surfaced

**Key milestone:** Champion uses Nexdata data in an actual IC memo and it adds value the team hadn't seen before.

### Stage 4: Internal Champion Builds the Case (Weeks 8-10)

**What the champion presents to COO/CFO:**
- Side-by-side: PitchBook output vs. Nexdata output on the same company
- Time savings calculation (hours saved × deal team hourly cost)
- Unique signals discovered (job posting trends, org chart changes, regulatory filings)
- Pilot usage data (how many queries, which features used)
- Risk mitigation (complementary to PitchBook, not replacement — easier to approve)

### Stage 5: Decision (Weeks 10-16)

**For mid-market firms (<$50K contract):**
- COO/CFO approves, contract signed
- Annual upfront payment, 5-8% annual escalator
- Go-live with full team rollout

**For larger firms (>$50K):**
- Partner meeting review
- Legal/procurement review (1-4 weeks)
- Contract negotiation

**Total cycle: 2-4 months for mid-market, 4-8 months for large**
