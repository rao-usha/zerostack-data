## Market Context

The private markets data and intelligence sector is undergoing rapid consolidation and AI-driven transformation. Key dynamics in 2025-2026:

- **BlackRock acquired Preqin** for $3.2B (closed March 2025), citing an $8B TAM growing to $18B by 2030
- **AlphaSense acquired Tegus** for $930M (closed July 2024), creating a $500M+ ARR intelligence juggernaut at $4B valuation
- **Datasite** (backed by CapVest's $500M commitment) acquired both **Grata** ($200M, June 2025) and **SourceScrub** (August 2025), assembling a full-stack origination-to-execution platform
- **S&P Global acquired Visible Alpha** (May 2024) and **With Intelligence** ($1.8B), folding both into Capital IQ Pro
- **FactSet acquired Cobalt GP** (October 2021) and **LiquidityBook** ($246.5M, February 2025)
- AI startup funding in Q1 2026 hit $242B (80% of all global venture), with vertical AI platforms among the hottest categories
- PE firms increasingly demand integrated platforms over point solutions

## Industry Landscape & Consolidation

### The Great Roll-Up (2024-2026)

The private markets data space is consolidating rapidly. Major moves:

| Acquirer | Target | Price | Date | Strategic Logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BlackRock | Preqin | $3.2B | Mar 2025 | Private markets data + Aladdin integration |
| AlphaSense | Tegus | $930M | Jul 2024 | Expert content + AI search platform |
| S&P Global | Visible Alpha | Undisclosed | May 2024 | Consensus models into Capital IQ Pro |
| S&P Global | With Intelligence | $1.8B | 2024 | Asset management intelligence |
| Datasite | Grata | ~$200M | Jun 2025 | AI private company search |
| Datasite | SourceScrub | Undisclosed | Aug 2025 | Human-enriched deal sourcing data |
| FactSet | Cobalt GP | Undisclosed | Oct 2021 | PE portfolio monitoring |
| FactSet | LiquidityBook | $246.5M | Feb 2025 | Order/execution management |
| Intapp | DealCloud | Undisclosed | Aug 2018 | PE deal CRM |
| Marlin Equity | Altvia | Undisclosed | Jul 2022 | Fund management software |

**Pattern:** Large incumbents (BlackRock, S&P, FactSet, Datasite) are buying specialized point solutions to build integrated platforms. This creates both threat (well-funded competitors) and opportunity (Nexdata as an acquisition target or partner for companies looking to add intelligence capabilities).

### PE Tech Stack Layers

Based on PE Stack and 2080 Ventures mapping, the PE tech stack has distinct layers:

1. **Data & Intelligence** (where Nexdata primarily competes): PitchBook, AlphaSense, Preqin, Grata, SourceScrub
2. **Deal CRM & Workflow**: DealCloud, Affinity, Rings AI
3. **Portfolio Monitoring**: Chronograph, Cobalt, 73 Strings
4. **Fund Operations**: Altvia, Carta, Allvue
5. **Execution**: Datasite VDR, Intralinks
6. **Wealth/LP**: Addepar, iCapital
7. **Specialized**: Bitsight (cyber DD), Neudata (alt data marketplace)

## Strategic Implications

### Positioning Options

1. **"Bloomberg Terminal for private markets"** -- High ambition, head-to-head with AlphaSense/PitchBook. Risky but massive TAM.

2. **"AI operating partner platform"** -- Differentiated positioning as a service, not just a tool. Combines data with advisory. Avoids head-to-head with incumbents.

3. **"Alternative data layer for PE"** -- Positions as a data provider that integrates with existing CRMs (DealCloud, Affinity) and analytics (PitchBook, Chronograph). Lower risk, faster distribution.

### Recommended Positioning

**Option 2 is the strongest** given the founder profile (ex-McKinsey + ex-Two Sigma) and the market dynamics. Key argument:

- PE firms don't want another tool -- they want better outcomes
- An operating partner backed by proprietary AI and data is a differentiated offering
- Retainer + carry alignment avoids the SaaS pricing death spiral
- The platform is the moat that enables the service
