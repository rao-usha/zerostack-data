# Operating Partner Ecosystem Research for Nexdata GTM

**Date:** April 6, 2026
**Purpose:** Map the operating partner ecosystem as a GTM channel and buyer segment for Nexdata
**Author:** Research compiled from industry knowledge, public sources, and competitive analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Operating partners are the fastest-growing role in private equity. The number of PE operating professionals has grown 3-4x over the past decade, with most top-50 PE firms now maintaining dedicated value creation teams of 10-50+ people. These professionals are:

1. **The ideal champion** for an AI intelligence platform — they sit between deal teams and portfolio companies, need data to drive value creation, and are measured on portfolio performance improvements
2. **Budget holders** — many have dedicated budgets for portfolio company tools and services
3. **Network multipliers** — one operating partner typically works with 5-15 portfolio companies, each of which could become a user
4. **McKinsey alumni magnets** — a disproportionate number came from McKinsey, Bain, and BCG, making them natural warm outreach targets for your network

This document maps seven buyer segments across the operating partner ecosystem with specific firms, people, conferences, and approach strategies.

- **Buyer identification:** When they have a sell-side mandate, they need to identify potential acquirers (strategic and financial). Nexdata's buyer discovery endpoint does this automatically
- **Client pitches:** They pitch PE firms with market intelligence and sector analysis. Nexdata can power those pitches
- **Competitive advantage:** In a commoditized advisory business, proprietary data/intelligence is a differentiator. Firms that can show clients unique insights win mandates
- **Current tools:** PitchBook (universal), Capital IQ, DealCloud CRM, Excel models, LinkedIn
- **Budget:** Mid-market banks spend $200K-$1M/year on data tools; boutiques spend $50-200K
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium-high — boutiques are cost-conscious but will pay for tools that help them win mandates

### Approach strategy

- **Best channel:** Direct outreach to Managing Directors who lead sector practices
- **Entry point:** "I can show you which companies in [their sector] are showing early signs of wanting to transact — hiring a CFO, adding board members, increasing compliance staff — all from public data"

- **Partnership model:** Some boutiques might white-label Nexdata as part of their advisory platform (like how some banks use SourceScrub/Grata)
- **Volume play:** Each bank has 20-50+ bankers; start with 2-3 seats, expand to team-wide
