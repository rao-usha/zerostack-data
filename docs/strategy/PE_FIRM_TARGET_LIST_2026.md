## COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT OPPORTUNITIES

### PitchBook Pain Points (Exploit These)

1. **Price:** $12K-$40K/user/year. Mid-market firms with 3-5 seats = $60-200K/year. Nexdata at $10-20K/mo for unlimited seats is compelling.
2. **Stale data:** PitchBook profiles are retrospective — "what has already happened." Nexdata is forward-looking (job postings, building permits, regulatory filings = leading indicators).
3. **No industrial depth:** PitchBook has no EPA, OSHA, EIA, BLS county-level, FMCSA, or utility rate data. Industrial PE firms cobble this together from consultants.
4. **No site intelligence:** PitchBook has zero county-level scoring for site selection (datacenter, manufacturing, logistics).
5. **No CRM integration:** PitchBook doesn't natively integrate with CRM/deal management systems, creating manual handoffs.
6. **Everyone sees the same list:** Since every subscriber sees the same companies, PitchBook isn't useful for proprietary deal sourcing.

### Capital IQ Pain Points

1. **Dated interface:** Steeper learning curve, less intuitive than modern tools.
2. **Public company focus:** Less useful for PE firms evaluating private middle-market companies.
3. **No alternative data:** No job postings, no reviews, no operational data.
