## 2. Pricing Models That Work in PE

### Model Comparison

| Model | Works For | Doesn't Work For | PE Fit |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Per-seat** | Data platforms (PitchBook, Grata) | Small firms (<5 people) | HIGH -- standard model |
| **Per-firm (flat)** | CRMs, workflow tools (DealCloud) | Large firms (overpay) | MEDIUM -- works for SMB |
| **Usage-based** | API/data feeds, compute | Predictability-minded buyers | LOW -- PE wants fixed costs |
| **Hybrid (seat + usage)** | Best-in-class NRR platforms | Simple products | HIGHEST -- best expansion |
| **Module-based** | Deep platforms (Preqin, Cap IQ) | Narrow products | HIGH -- natural upsell |

### Recommended Model for Nexdata

**Primary:** Per-seat with module upsell  
**Structure:**
- Base platform fee (per seat) -- core search, company intelligence, job posting signals
- Add-on modules -- DD automation, site intelligence, LP/GP universe, portfolio monitoring
- Usage-based overlay -- LLM-powered research queries, API calls, custom reports

**Why this works:**
1. PE firms budget per-seat (matches their mental model)
2. Module expansion drives NRR above 120%
3. Usage metering on AI features aligns cost with value and captures power users
4. Hybrid models deliver best-in-class NRR (per McKinsey + Benchmarkit data)

### Sweet Spot for Mid-Market PE ($1-5B AUM)

- **Team size:** 5-15 investment professionals + 2-5 ops/IR
- **Budget per data tool:** $20K-$60K/yr (small line item vs. management fees)
- **Pain threshold:** >$100K/yr requires CFO/Managing Partner approval
- **Sweet spot entry price:** $15K-$25K/yr for 2-3 seats
- **Expansion target:** $40K-$80K/yr within 12-18 months

### Contract Structure

- **Annual contracts:** Standard (PE firms almost universally do annual)
- **Multi-year:** 2-year contracts with 10-15% discount; 3-year with 20-25% discount
- **Payment terms:** Annual upfront (PE firms prefer this; improves your cash flow)
- **Auto-renewal:** Include 5-8% annual escalator (industry standard)
- **Cancellation:** 90-day notice required; no mid-term cancellation

### Free Trial / Freemium in PE

Freemium generally does NOT work for PE data tools because:
- High per-user cost to deliver (data licensing, LLM inference)
- Small target market (~4,000 PE firms in the US)
- Complex product requires onboarding
- Decision-makers don't self-serve

**What DOES work:**
- **Time-limited pilot (30-60 days):** Full product, 1-2 seats, with dedicated onboarding
- **"Proof of value" engagement:** Run a specific analysis on their actual portfolio/pipeline
- **Conference demo:** Show their own data back to them (use Nexdata's 28+ sources)
- **Free report/intel:** Monthly market intelligence email that demonstrates data quality

## 4. Go-to-Market Approaches

### How Successful PE Tech Companies Got Their First 50 Customers

**Grata (founded 2016, $34.5M raised, $200M exit):**
- Harvard network (founders are Harvard alums) for warm intros to PE firms
- Product-first: built genuinely differentiated private company search before selling
- Started with lower mid-market PE firms (less price-sensitive, faster decisions)
- Conference presence + case studies (e.g., First Analysis, DWP Capital, Normandy)
- Grew ARR 8x in 2021 once product-market fit was strong

**Tegus (founded 2017, $108M raised, $930M acquisition by AlphaSense):**
- Expert network model: built supply (experts) and demand (investors) simultaneously
- Targeted hedge fund analysts first (high willingness to pay, fast decision cycle)
- Content-driven: published expert transcripts as marketing (showed quality)
- Expanded from hedge funds to PE to corporate strategy

**Harmonic.ai (B2B intelligence, $30M raised):**
- VC-first: targeted venture investors who need startup discovery
- Data quality as differentiator (company tracking, signal detection)
- Community-driven: "Hot 25" quarterly reports as content marketing
- PLG elements: free tier for individual VCs, team pricing for firms

### Content Marketing That Works in PE

**What PE professionals actually read:**
- **Market intelligence reports** (e.g., "Q2 2026 Middle Market Deal Activity by Sector")
- **Data-driven insights** (e.g., "Job Posting Signals Predict Revenue Growth 6 Months Early")
- **Case studies** with real numbers (e.g., "How [Firm] sourced 15 deals in 12 months")
- **Sector-specific deep dives** (infrastructure, healthcare, tech services)

**What does NOT convert:**
- Generic blog posts about "digital transformation in PE"
- Listicles or shallow content
- Pure product marketing without data/insight

**Distribution channels that work:**
- LinkedIn (PE professionals are heavy LinkedIn users)
- Email newsletters (monthly cadence, high-signal content)
- Industry publications (PE Hub, Buyouts, Pitchbook News)
- Podcast guest spots (The PEVC Show, Capital Allocators, Privcap)

### The "Champion" Inside a PE Firm

**Who is the champion?**
- **VP or Principal** on the deal sourcing team (power user, lives in data tools daily)
- NOT the Managing Partner (too senior to evaluate), NOT the Analyst (too junior to buy)

**Champion profile:**
- 28-35 years old, 5-10 years in PE
- Frustrated with PitchBook's limitations on private company data
- Has tried building Excel-based workflows and wants something better
- Motivated by deal flow efficiency (more proprietary deals = faster path to Partner)

**How to activate the champion:**
1. Show them something they can't get elsewhere (e.g., job posting intelligence on their target companies)
2. Give them a "proof of value" pilot on their actual pipeline
3. Make them look good internally (provide data for an IC memo that impresses the MD)
4. Arm them with ROI talking points for the budget conversation

**Buying process in PE firms:**
1. VP/Principal identifies need and evaluates tools (2-4 weeks)
2. Shares with deal team, gets 2-3 users to trial (2-4 weeks)
3. Champion presents to COO/CFO for budget approval (1-2 weeks)
4. Annual budget cycle review (timing matters: Q4 budget planning for Q1 start)
5. Legal/procurement review (1-2 weeks for smaller firms, 4-8 weeks for large)
6. **Total cycle: 2-4 months for mid-market; 4-8 months for large firms**

### Pricing Recommendation

| Tier | Price/Seat/Year | Target | Includes |
|------|----------------|--------|----------|
| **Starter** | $10,000 | Small PE, Corp Dev | Company search, job signals, basic alerts |

**vs. PitchBook ($24K+/seat):** Nexdata offers AI-native research automation + job posting intelligence that PitchBook doesn't have, at similar or lower price point. "PitchBook gives you data. Nexdata gives you analysis."

**vs. Capital IQ ($18K+/seat):** Nexdata is purpose-built for PE workflows vs. Cap IQ's broad financial services approach. AI-native vs. legacy platform.

2. **Months 7-12:** Hire first AE, ramp conference pipeline, close 10 more ($500K incremental)
